Our Story: From Empty Lot to Thriving Community Hub

Discover how five determined families transformed unused land into a flourishing garden that now serves over 200 households in Benoni.

Home > About Us
2019

The Beginning

Five families in Benoni North noticed an unused municipal lot filled with weeds and litter. Instead of complaining, they decided to act. With nothing but determination and a few gardening tools, they began clearing the land and planting their first vegetables.

2020

Growing Community

Word spread through the neighborhood, and by the end of 2020, 25 families were actively participating. We established our first composting system and built raised beds. The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the importance of local food security, drawing even more community interest.

2021

Official Recognition

Ekurhuleni Municipality officially recognized our initiative and granted us a 10-year lease on the land. We registered as a non-profit organization and received our first grant funding. Our first school visit welcomed 30 Grade 3 learners from Benoni Primary.

2022

Educational Expansion

We launched formal educational programs, partnering with 8 local schools. Our first annual Harvest Festival attracted over 300 community members. We installed rainwater harvesting systems and expanded to 50 garden plots.

2023

Sustainable Growth

We achieved food security for 120 families through our expanded plots. Solar panels were installed for our tool shed and educational center. Our youth program began training the next generation of environmental stewards.

2024-2025

Community Hub

Today, Green Future serves 200+ families with 150+ active volunteers. We've expanded to include a seed library, community kitchen, and host 12 partner schools. Our model is being replicated in three other Gauteng communities.
